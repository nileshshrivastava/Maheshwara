# Maheshwara Project Manifesto

> **Nothing is assumed. Everything is derived.**

---

# Purpose

This manifesto defines the principles that govern every research decision, software implementation, experiment, and contribution within the Maheshwara Project.

These principles exist to ensure that the project remains faithful to its source, transparent in its methods, and reproducible in its results.

---

# Principle 1 — The Source Comes First

The Maheshwara Sutras are the primary source of this project.

Every computational structure must ultimately be traceable to the sutras themselves or to a documented experiment performed upon them.

No external theory takes precedence over the source.

---

# Principle 2 — Observation Before Interpretation

Every investigation follows the same order:

Observation

↓

Documentation

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Experiment

↓

Conclusion

Interpretation is never allowed to precede observation.

---

# Principle 3 — Preserve Before Simplifying

The first responsibility of the project is faithful preservation.

Information should never be removed merely because it appears redundant or inconvenient.

Reduction is permitted only after it has been demonstrated that no information is lost.

---

# Principle 4 — Nothing is Added Without Evidence

No computational field, relationship, classification, or rule may be introduced simply because it appears reasonable.

Every addition must satisfy at least one of the following:

* It exists explicitly in the Maheshwara Sutras.
* It is derived through a documented algorithm.
* It is declared as a hypothesis.

---

# Principle 5 — Derived Knowledge is Never Stored

If information can be reproduced algorithmically from the Canonical Maheshwara Representation, it should not be permanently stored.

The representation remains minimal.

Knowledge is generated through computation.

---

# Principle 6 — The Canonical Representation is Immutable

The Canonical Maheshwara Representation (CMR) is the foundation of the project.

Algorithms may observe it.

Algorithms may derive information from it.

Algorithms shall never modify it.

---

# Principle 7 — Every Assumption Must Be Explicit

Every statement introduced into the repository belongs to one of the following categories:

* Observation
* Hypothesis
* Experiment
* Conclusion
* Implementation Choice

These categories shall never be confused.

---

# Principle 8 — Experiments Decide

Questions are resolved through reproducible experiments rather than preference or authority.

A convincing experiment has greater value than an appealing assumption.

---

# Principle 9 — Documentation is Part of the Research

Documentation is not supplementary.

Documentation is part of the scientific process.

Every important decision, experiment, and architectural change should be recorded.

Future researchers should be able to understand not only what was built, but why it was built.

---

# Principle 10 — Traceability

Every major component of the project should be traceable back through the following chain:

Maheshwara Sutra

↓

Observation

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Specification

↓

Implementation

↓

Experiment

↓

Conclusion

Nothing should exist without an identifiable origin.

---

# Principle 11 — Implementation Serves the Research

Programming languages, data structures, and software architectures are implementation tools.

They are not the source of truth.

The structure of the Maheshwara Sutras guides the computational architecture—not the other way around.

---

# Principle 12 — Simplicity is Earned

Complexity shall never be introduced prematurely.

Whenever multiple representations are possible, preference should be given to the one requiring the fewest assumptions while preserving complete information.

---

# Principle 13 — Every Result Must Be Reproducible

A result has value only when another researcher can independently reproduce it using the documented source, specifications, and experiments.

Reproducibility is the standard of acceptance.

---

# Principle 14 — Negative Results are Knowledge

Failed experiments shall be documented.

Rejected hypotheses remain valuable because they prevent future researchers from repeating the same path.

Knowledge includes understanding what does not work.

---

# Principle 15 — The Project is Larger Than Its Founders

The Maheshwara Project is intended to become a lasting body of computational research.

Contributors may change.

Implementations may evolve.

Hypotheses may be refined.

The commitment to disciplined inquiry, transparency, and faithful representation shall remain constant.

---

# Research Workflow

Every investigation follows the same lifecycle:

Observation

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Experiment

↓

Result

↓

Conclusion

↓

Law (when repeatedly confirmed)

No stage may be skipped.

---

# Research Values

The Maheshwara Project values:

* Curiosity over certainty.
* Evidence over assumption.
* Simplicity over unnecessary complexity.
* Transparency over hidden implementation.
* Reproducibility over convenience.
* Collaboration over ownership.

---

# Motto

> **Observe faithfully. Preserve completely. Derive algorithmically.**

---

# Closing Statement

The Maheshwara Project is founded upon the belief that careful observation, disciplined experimentation, and honest documentation can reveal computational structures that are worthy of study in their own right.

This manifesto does not define what the Maheshwara Sutras mean.

It defines how we shall approach the question.

Every future contribution to this repository should strengthen these principles and preserve the integrity of the research.
